A single Viceroy tulip — a regal purple variety that was among the most sought-after during the tulip mania. Style: rich oil painting with visible impasto brushstrokes and warm golden lighting, maintaining the clarity of botanical illustration but with the depth and warmth of a Dutch Golden Age still life. The tulip is shown at slight angle, petals fully open, displayed against a pure warm dark brown background. The flower features deep royal purple petals with dramatic white feathering and streaking at the petal edges, creating an aristocratic contrast. The purple is rich and velvety — not flat or cartoonish — with subtle gradients from dark plum at the base to lighter violet at the ruffled petal tips. White streaks are bold and painterly. A single strong green stem with broad curving leaves. The flower conveys nobility and elegance — it looks expensive. Warm dramatic lighting from the upper left.

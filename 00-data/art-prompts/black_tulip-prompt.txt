A single Black Tulip — an impossibly rare and almost mythical variety, the holy grail of 1637 tulip collectors. Style: rich oil painting with visible impasto brushstrokes and warm golden lighting, maintaining the clarity of botanical illustration but with the depth and warmth of a Dutch Golden Age still life. The tulip is shown at slight angle, petals fully open, displayed against a pure warm dark brown background. The flower features petals of such deep dark purple-maroon that they appear truly black in most lighting — but here, under warm candlelight, subtle undertones of deep amethyst and midnight purple reveal themselves at the petal edges and in the subtle folds. The darkness is luxurious and velvety, not flat black. Petal edges catch a faint highlight of warm gold from the candlelight, creating an almost supernatural shimmer. A single dark green stem with slightly darker leaves than the other varieties. A tiny dewdrop on one petal reflects the warm light like a dark jewel. The flower should feel rare, mysterious, and slightly dangerous — as if it holds forbidden value. Warm dramatic lighting from the upper left. ABSOLUTELY NO TEXT, no letters, no numbers, no labels, no specimen tags, no signatures. Pure flower study on solid dark background.
